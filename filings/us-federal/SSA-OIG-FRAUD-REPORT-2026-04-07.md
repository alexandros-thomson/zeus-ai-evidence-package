### IV. THE $24,016 FORM 1116 FALSE CREDIT CHAIN

This section documents the downstream U.S. tax fraud that the phantom AFM enabled, providing context for SSA's coordination with IRS-CI.

H&R Block preparers Weissgerber (PTIN P00694437) and Worman then entered those Greek payment records on Form 1116 (Foreign Tax Credit) on U.S. federal tax returns for tax years 2021 through 2024. Two categories of payments were entered as creditable foreign taxes:

- **Ghost income tax (€1,320.24):** This income tax was wrongly assessed — it was generated by ghost E1 filings on a dead man's identity and should never have existed. It cannot be a creditable foreign income tax.

**The result:** $24,016 in confirmed false foreign tax credits were issued by the IRS. The confirmed figure is net of any legitimate credits; the ceiling of fraudulent claims is approximately $30,000 over TY 2021–2024. The Form 1116 that generated these credits contains a mathematically impossible figure: -$830,000 in foreign income, which is the arithmetic consequence of inflating the foreign tax credit base approximately 5× beyond actual Greek payments. ### V. FATCA/CRS ANALYSIS — SSA DEATH NOTIFICATION IMPLICATIONS

Greece is a FATCA Model 1 IGA partner. Under FATCA obligations codified in the U.S.-Greece Intergovernmental Agreement, Greek financial institutions and AADE are required to automatically exchange information with the IRS on U.S. persons' financial accounts, including reportable events such as the death of a U.S. person.

The FATCA reporting chain for a deceased U.S. person is:

1. Greek financial institution / AADE detects death of U.S. person
2. FATCA-required report transmitted to IRS
3. IRS updates death master file
4. SSA receives death notification through standard IRS-SSA data exchange

**The failure:** AFM 051422558 was never deactivated. As of April 6, 2026 — 1,760 days after John Kyprianos's death — it remains active in AADE's TAXISnet system. If AADE's FATCA reporting systems rely on AFM status to trigger U.S.-person death reporting, the phantom active AFM may have suppressed or indefinitely delayed FATCA death notification for SSN 369-60-0194.

**Every FATCA/CRS information exchange involving AFM 051422558 from June 13, 2021 onward reported a deceased U.S. citizen as alive.** The NBG (National Bank of Greece) account associated with the estate recorded 438 transactions post-mortem. Under FATCA, those account transactions and the account holder's status should have been reported to the IRS as involving a deceased U.S. person. If they were reported as involving a living U.S. person — because the phantom AFM remained active — every one of those reports was a false FATCA filing.

**Treaty framework violated:**
- U.S.-Greece FATCA Model 1 IGA (operative information exchange obligations)
- U.S.-Greece 1953 Tax Convention, Art. XVII/XVIII/XIX (post-mortem obligations on U.S. citizen estate)
- U.S.-Greece 1953 Tax Convention, Art. 26 (information exchange)
- U.S.-Greece 1950 Estate Tax Convention

**SSA question requiring investigation:** When did SSA receive notification of John Kyprianos's death (SSN 369-60-0194)? The death certificate was filed in Macomb County, Michigan in June 2021. If SSA received timely domestic notification through the standard death registration → Social Security Death Index pathway, the FATCA failure may not have caused SSA records to be contaminated. However, if any benefit determination for John Kyprianos or Stamatina Kyprianos relied on foreign tax record data (income, residency, or asset information), the false Greek records may have affected those determinations.

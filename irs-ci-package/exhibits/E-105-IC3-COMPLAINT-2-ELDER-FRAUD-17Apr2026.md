### 5. DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT

ELDER FINANCIAL EXPLOITATION — CROSS-BORDER IDENTITY THEFT AND FRAUD

Stamatina Kyprianos is a 79-year-old US citizen, widow of Ioannis (John) Kyprianos, a US Navy and Hellenic Navy/NATO veteran who died on June 13, 2021 in Macomb County, Michigan. Since his death — 1,771 days ago — a coordinated scheme has operated across Greece and the United States to exploit both the deceased veteran's identity and his elderly surviving spouse's financial accounts.

THE GREEK SIDE:

1. IDENTITY THEFT (15 YEARS): The primary subject, Efthalia Kyprianos (AFM 052822816), designated herself as unauthorized tax representative for both the victim (AFM 044594747) and the deceased (AFM 051422558), using email misthwtos2012@hotmail.com and phone 6940578705 to access their TAXISnet (Greek tax portal) accounts from approximately 2011 to 2026. AADE's own complaints unit (DESYP) formally acknowledged the unauthorized access in Protocol G 87848.

2. POSTHUMOUS BANK FRAUD: 438 separate transactions totaling EUR 21,616 were executed on the deceased's NBG account (IBAN GR03 0110 4700 0000 4707 4777 757) between 13/06/2021 and 05/04/2026. None were authorized by any legal heir. Each transaction traversed the SWIFT/TARGET2/SEPA banking network, establishing US wire fraud jurisdiction under 18 USC 1343.

3. PHANTOM TAX FILINGS: Three income tax returns (E1 declarations) were filed on the deceased's active AFM 051422558 for tax years 2022, 2023, and 2024, generating EUR 1,320.24 in tax obligations that were then cross-charged to the widow's AFM 044594747. Someone logged into TAXISnet as or on behalf of a dead man and filed tax returns for three consecutive years.

4. PHANTOM PROPERTY REGISTRATIONS: Cadastral record KAEK 050681726008 (Vosporou 14, Keratsini, 187 55) declares 6 apartments totaling 359 sq.m. on a single-story ruined building of 231 sq.m. total surface. Google Street View imagery (2011-2024) confirms the building remained single-story and undivided throughout. This constitutes forgery of cadastral records (Greek Penal Code Art. 216, 242).

5. RETALIATORY TAX DEMAND: On the same day AADE/DESYP acknowledged identity theft (G 87848), AADE's enforcement division issued a EUR 945,000 demand against the victim Stamatina Kyprianos — based on property sale proceeds attributed to Efthalia Kyprianos, the same person AADE had just removed as unauthorized tax representative. Coordination or catastrophic institutional failure — either answer indicates systemic harm to the victim.

6. PENSION VOID: Three Greek pension authorities (HDYKA, e-EFKA, NAT) have each disclaimed responsibility for the deceased's Hellenic Navy pension. HDYKA says zero in their system. EFKA says not their competence. NAT distributed the inquiry to 4 directorates. The deceased's own 1981 Greek ID card bears the stamp SYNTAXIOUXOS (pensioner). Someone has been collecting his pension. His widow never received the survivor's benefit she is entitled to under Greek law.

THE US SIDE:

7. FALSE TAX CREDITS: H&R Block's preparer Mellissa Worman filed Form 1116 Foreign Tax Credit claims for TY 2021-2024 totaling approximately $30,000 against actual foreign tax payments of $5,984 — producing $24,016 in false credits. The IRS has accepted the amended return (1040-X) correcting the false credits to zero.

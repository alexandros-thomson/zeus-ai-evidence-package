## 1. PURPOSE

This petition requests that IRS-CI initiate a formal MLAT Article 9 request through the U.S. Department of Justice Office of International Affairs (DOJ OIA) to compel the National Bank of Greece (NBG) to produce the complete payment ledger for accounts associated with the deceased taxpayer Ioannis (John) Kyprianos (AFM 051422558) and his widow Stamatina Kyprianos (AFM 044594747).

## 2. WHY NBG IS THE STRONGEST MLAT ASK

NBG holds the single most critical evidentiary chain in this case:

1. **438 post-mortem transactions** totaling EUR 21,616 on AFM 051422558 — who authorized them, who received the funds, and under what authority
2. **Stamatina's outgoing payments** — she paid DEH, COSMOTE, ENFIA, and other obligations through NBG in a dead man's name because the notary (Lyrakis) blocked the legal transfer, forcing her to maintain the deceased AFM as the only payment path
3. **IBAN GR0301104700000047074777757** — the account that processed these transactions
4. **Ghost income tax extractions** — EUR 1,303.55 over 3 confirmed tax years extracted from Stamatina's account based on fraudulent tax obligations on the deceased AFM
5. **The EUR 35 e-Paravolo payment** — the forensic anchor that traces to notary Lyrakis and unmasks the AIT.1 actor

The treaty permits the Requesting State (U.S.) to obtain banking records, financial documents, and testimony from the Requested State (Greece) for use in criminal investigations and proceedings.

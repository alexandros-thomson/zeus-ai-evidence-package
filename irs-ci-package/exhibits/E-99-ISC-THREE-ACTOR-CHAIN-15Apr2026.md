### 3. Why -$830,000 Is Impossible

**Form 1116 mechanics:** Form 1116 (Foreign Tax Credit) Line 1a requires the taxpayer to report gross income from foreign sources in the relevant income category. This must be a positive figure representing actual foreign-source earnings, rents, royalties, pensions, or other income actually received from outside the United States.

**The impossibility:** A figure of -$830,000 on Line 1a asserts that the taxpayer sustained an $830,000 net loss from foreign operations during the tax year. For this to be legitimate, the taxpayer would need to have had $830,000 in documented foreign-source losses — from a foreign business, foreign investments, foreign employment, or similar foreign economic activity generating a net negative result of that magnitude.

**Stamatina Kyprianos's actual profile:**
- Retired widow, age 79
- Zero foreign business activity
- Zero foreign employment
- Zero foreign investment portfolio
- Foreign income consists exclusively of ENFIA (Greek real property tax obligations), not income-generating activity
- Actual Greek tax paid (ENFIA): $5,984

There is no mathematical, factual, or legal scenario under which a 79-year-old retired widow with no foreign income-producing activity could have -$830,000 in foreign source income. The figure is not aggressive tax planning. It is not a debatable position. It is a computational impossibility under IRC §901 and the Form 1116 instructions.

**IRS confirmation:** The IRS accepted the amended return (Form 1040-X, TY2024, Submission 3891692026100017w2tu, documented at Exhibit E-84) correcting the foreign source income to $0. The agency's acceptance is dispositive. The figure was wrong. It has been corrected. Three H&R Block actors defended it after the fact.

**The FTC inflation:** Separately, the Form 1116 also inflated the Foreign Tax Credit claimed from $5,984 (actual ENFIA paid) to $30,000 — a 500% inflation. This is documented across tax years 2021–2024 at a total false credit amount of $24,016 (Exhibit E-90).

### 6. Legal Significance

**IRC §7434 — Civil Damages for Fraudulent Filing of Information Returns**
Three actors across three organizational levels (preparer, administrative review, outside counsel) have now independently authenticated the -$830,000 figure as H&R Block's position. This pattern eliminates the "isolated preparer error" defense. A single preparer filing a wrong figure is an error. That same figure surviving executive escalation review (Cox) and then being defended on the record by outside counsel at a formal AAA conference (Wahl) is an institutional pattern. Under IRC §7434, willful or reckless conduct in filing false information supports statutory damages of the greater of $5,000 or actual damages. The three-actor chain supports the willfulness inference.

**AAA Consumer Rule R-47 — Punitive Damages**
Rule R-47 permits the arbitrator to award punitive damages upon a finding of conduct that is willful, wanton, or in reckless disregard of the claimant's rights. Wahl's affirmative ISC defense of a figure the IRS has already corrected, combined with the prior Cox ratification and Worman origination, constitutes the multi-layer pattern that R-47 punitive claims require. The $100,000 punitive line item in the amended demand is now supported by three independent authentication events.

**26 USC §7206(2) — Aiding and Assisting in Preparation of False Return**
Each of the three actors is independently citable under §7206(2) for their role in preparing, approving, or defending a materially false tax document. Worman prepared it. Cox approved it upon review. Wahl defended it in a legal proceeding. The downstream citation potential for the IRS-CI binder (Tab 5, Lane 1) is documented.

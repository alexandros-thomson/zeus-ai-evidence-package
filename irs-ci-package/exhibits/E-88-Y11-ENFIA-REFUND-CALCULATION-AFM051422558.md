## Preliminary Notes

ENFIA (Ενιαίος Φόρος Ιδιοκτησίας Ακινήτων) is assessed annually by AADE against the registered owner of each property as of **1 January of each tax year**. The legal position is therefore:

1. A taxpayer who dies on 13 June 2021 is the legitimate ENFIA-payer for **1 January 2021** (death falls within the 2021 assessment year). Under Greek civil and tax law, his estate (and ultimately his heirs) bear the 2021 ENFIA liability in principle — **but only the proportional portion up to the date of death, unless the estate specifically accepts the full-year obligation.**
2. As of **1 January 2022** and every subsequent year, John Kyprianos had no legal existence as a taxpayer. Any ENFIA assessment issued against AFM 051422558 for years 2022, 2023, and 2024 is a **legally void phantom obligation**.
3. KAEK 050681726008 (Keratsini) does not appear in the E9 declaration of AFM 044594747, which is the only E9 available for the family's real estate portfolio. Its assessment against a dead taxpayer's AFM compounds the phantom character.
4. The εκκαθαριστικά figure of **€3,045.83 in annual real estate income** signals that the AADE system continued treating AFM 051422558 as an active income-generating entity through at least tax year 2024.

## E. Legal Basis for Recovery

### E.1 Art. 32 Ν.4172/2013 — Income Tax Code: Death Terminates Tax Obligations

Article 32 of the Greek Income Tax Code (Κώδικας Φορολογίας Εισοδήματος) establishes that tax obligations are personal to the natural person taxpayer. Upon death, the tax legal personality of the deceased is extinguished. **Tax assessments issued against a deceased taxpayer's AFM for periods commencing after death are legally void ab initio** — not merely voidable. The AADE system's continued issuance of ENFIA assessments on AFM 051422558 for tax years 2022, 2023, and 2024 constitutes a systemic administrative error with no valid legal foundation.

### E.2 Art. 63 §2 Ν.4987/2022 — Refund of Unduly Assessed Taxes

Law 4987/2022 (Tax Procedures Code recodification) expressly provides, at Article 63 §2, that **taxes collected without legal obligation** are refundable with statutory interest. The procedure requires:

1. Filing of an amended/corrective E9 or a formal objection (ενδικοφανής προσφυγή) to AADE within the applicable limitation period.
2. Submission of death certificate as documentary evidence that the AFM was that of a deceased person.
3. AADE must issue a corrective determination within 120 days; failure to respond triggers deemed refusal subject to administrative court review.

The **εκκαθαριστικά showing €3,045.83 real estate income** issued post-mortem further documents that AADE continued treating the deceased as an active taxpayer — this record is itself exhibit evidence for the §2 refund claim.

### E.3 ECHR Protocol 1, Article 1 — Protection of Property

The continued assessment of ENFIA on a deceased taxpayer's estate without legal basis, and the consequent extraction of funds from the estate or heirs, constitutes an **unjustified interference with the peaceful enjoyment of possessions** within the meaning of ECHR Protocol 1, Article 1. Relevant ECtHR jurisprudence includes:

- *Iatridis v. Greece* (App. No. 31107/96): unlawful State interference with property not justified by public interest
- *Immobiliare Saffi v. Italy* (App. No. 22774/93): proportionality of tax burden on property owners
- *Gasus Dosier- und Fördertechnik GmbH v. the Netherlands* (App. No. 15375/89): scope of "possessions" in tax context

The **phantom KAEK 050681726008** — a property never declared on any E9, assessed for ENFIA against a dead AFM — presents the clearest Protocol 1 violation: there is no ownership nexus, no legal basis, and no proportionate public-interest justification.

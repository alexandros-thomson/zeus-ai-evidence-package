## Why This Matters for the Criminal Case

This record establishes the **earliest known date of identity compromise** — 26/05/1992, twenty-nine years before John's death and thirty-two years before any known actor in the current case (Lyrakis, Efthalia Kyprianou, Papageorgiou) entered the picture.

The phantom business registration is the **foundational act** enabling all subsequent exploitation:
- It proves a Greek business identity was created on a U.S. citizen's AFM without her knowledge or consent
- The 1992 date predates TAXISnet entirely — this was a physical DOY filing by someone who walked into an office
- The business remains ΕΝΕΡΓΗ in AADE's current registry despite ΠΑΥΣΗ ΕΡΓΑΣΙΩΝ logged at 31/12/1999

For the U.S. tax fraud case specifically: the phantom Greek business identity is what enabled the false Form 1116 Foreign Tax Credit claims ($24,016 overstated, 2021–2024) that are the subject of IRS CI Case 26236541 and AAA Arbitration Case 01-26-0001-2493. Without the phantom business creating the appearance of Greek tax obligations, the fabricated foreign tax credits on the U.S. returns have no source.

## Cross-AFM Finding (NEW — 31/03/2026)

The phantom business is registered on **AFM 044594747** (Stamatina), but AADE's automated Α.1058/2026 POS compliance notification went to **AFM 051422558** (John, deceased 13/06/2021). This cross-AFM routing means:

This is directly relevant to the false E1/E2 filings (2021–2024) on AFM 051422558 by Accountant Aggeliki Papageorgiou: the system infrastructure that enables cross-AFM confusion is the same infrastructure that was exploited to file false returns.
